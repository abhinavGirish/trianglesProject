So the triangles once again can be superimposed and are therefore congruent...this leads us to another method of establishing congruence, also known as SAS, or 'side-angle-side' congruence. In other words, if two triangles have corresponding sides that are of the same length along with corresponding included angles that are of the same measure, then these triangles are congruent.
