Voila! There's another triangle. If you think about it, the rays from the two angles can only intersect at one point and thus only one distinct triangle can be formed from these parts. Using similar logic with superimposition as in the previous two postulates, this leads us to another fundamental rule in geometry: the ASA or 'Angle-Side-Angle' postulate. In other words, two triangles are congruent if two corresponding angles and included sidelength are all congruent. There are a few exercises after the lesson for you to try on your own.
