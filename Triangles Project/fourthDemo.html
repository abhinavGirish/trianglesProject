So they can be superimposed...therefore, they are congruent...this leads to a postulate that is important in establishing congruence - if all three sidelengths of two triangles are congruent(which we know to be true, beginning with two sets of the same sidelengths above), then the triangles are congruent. This is also known as SSS, short for side-side-side congruence. In other words, if there are two triangles with corresponding sides that are the same in measure, then the two triangles are congruent.Let's take a look at a few other ways of establishing congruence...
